Restored GPG keys are invisible

Published on: 2024-12-30T23:34:50Z

Summary: I did an rsync -a backup of ~/.gnupg and an ensuing restore using the same method: $ ls -l ~/.gnupg/private-keys-v1.d/ total 24 -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3789 Jul 22 00:01 21CE5A648FC098016B210F19C5894CA090AC8FA0.key -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3787 Jul 22 00:01 29E446B279D1C67463DEA9546D2490DBC026E99B.key -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3787 Jul 22 00:01 3B5CEDC2D9BE791009DA185BAA4C80A9B1CE9996.key -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3789 Jul 22 00:01 3FC073BE0F3A6EDBFC7D28E07A0C287C7D9DB129.key -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3787 Jul 22 00:01 92CF31DD4DEA6DC2AB95E4E44D91DF191BF59E2A.key -rw——- 1 dustin dustin 3789 Jul 22 00:01 E7492BF0A909568CD2F40744E3BD4FC466825B4B.key (Its odd that they all have the same date, but at least its in the past.) Nothing is showing under –list-secret-keys. When I do "gpg –armor –export " with one of the IDs in the filenames above, it says "nothing exported". I dont really mind regenerating keys in this case, but Id like to understand whats going on here. All search results that I found on the matter were due either to permissions/ownership or, in one case, the GPG version. However, I spot-checked the permissions/owners and they looked right, and, given the rsync option, I wouldnt expect that to be an issue. Im [currently] using GPG 2.4.7 on Manjaro 24. I do not have access to the original environment, which was Pop OS (probably 22).
